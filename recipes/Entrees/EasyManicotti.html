Easy Manicotti

Contributed by Ron Paynter
INGREDIENTS

2 pounds ricotta cheese
12 oz (3 cups) grated mozzerarella cheese, divided
1/3 cup grated parmesan cheese
2 eggs
1 tsp salt
1/2 tsp ground black pepper
1 Tbsp chopped parsley
1 26-oz jar spaghetti sauce
1 14.5 oz can diced tomatoes with basil, garlic, and oregano, undrained
1 16-oz package egg roll wrappers (21 6x6 inch squares)

DIRECTIONS
  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. Combine ricotta, 2 cups grated mozarella, parmesan, eggs, salt, black pepper and parsley in a large bowl.
  3. Mix well.
  4. In another bowl, combine the spaghetti sauce with the diced tomatoes.
  5. Spray 2 9x13 inch glass baking dishes with nonstick spray.
  6. Spread 1 1/2 cups sauce in the bottom of each dish.
  7. Prepare the manicotti by placing an egg roll wrapper on a flat surface.
  8. Spoon 1/4 cup filling in the center of each and roll up.
  9. Continue preparing manicotti until all filling is used, placing them seam side down in a single layer in the sauced dishes.
  10. Spread 1 cup of sauce evenly over the manicotti in each dish.
  11. Cover dishes tightly with foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  12. Remove casseroles from oven and remove foil.
  13. Sprinkle each casserole with 1/2 cup of the remaining mozzarella cheese.
  14. Return to oven, uncovered, and bake an additional 10 minutes.
  15. Makes 10 to 12 servings.
  16. NOTE: The prepared, uncooked manicotti can be frozen in the baking dish, well covered with foil, for up to one month.
