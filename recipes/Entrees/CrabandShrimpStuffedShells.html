Crab and Shrimp Stuffed Shells

Contributed by Julie Williams
INGREDIENTS

20–24 jumbo pasta shells
1 cup cooked shrimp, finely chopped
1 cup cooked crab meat (or lobster), chopped
1 cup ricotta cheese
1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella, divided (1/2 cup filling + 1 cup sauce)
1 1/4 cups grated Parmesan, divided (1/4 cup filling + 1 cup sauce) • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
4 tbsp butter
4 tbsp all-purpose flour
3 cups heavy cream
1 cup + 2 tbsp half-and-half, divided (2 tbsp filling + 1 cup sauce)
Salt, to taste
Black pepper, to taste
1 cup shredded mozzarella (on top)
Fresh parsley or chives (optional)

DIRECTIONS
  1. Boil the jumbo shells just until al dente, then drain and let them cool a bit. They should feel firm enough to hold the filling.
  2. In a bowl, mix shrimp, crab, ricotta, cream cheese, 1/2 cup mozzarella, 1/4 cup Parmesan, Italian seasoning, and 2 tbsp half-and-half. Stir gently so it stays creamy and scoopable.
  3. In a saucepan, melt butter, whisk in flour, then slowly whisk in heavy cream and the remaining 1 cup half-and-half. Stir in 1 cup mozzarella and 1 cup Parmesan, then season with salt and black pepper until silky and thick.
  4. Spoon a thin layer of sauce into a baking dish, stuff the shells, and arrange them snugly. Pour the rest of the sauce over top and finish with 1 cup mozzarella (on top).
  5. Bake at 375°F covered for 20 minutes, then uncover for 10 minutes until bubbly and lightly golden. You want that cheesy top to look soft and melty.
  6. Rest 5 minutes, then sprinkle with parsley or chives if you like. Serve warm — this is pure weeknight comfort.
